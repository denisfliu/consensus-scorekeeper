Rules briefing for participants

The pack

  • 100 questions per pack.
  • Each pack is divided into a sequence of categories.
  • Most questions are worth 10 points; a few categories score differently.

The categories

Every pack mixes together questions from these category types. Each one has its own rules for how it plays.

Blitz Set Mystery Linked Set Double Jump Streak Jailbreak Split Jackpot

All examples in the next slides are taken from Consensus 2024-25 Post-Secondary T1 Packs.

Category

Blitz

A specified number of assorted-subject questions, all open to both teams. Each worth 10 points.

Example — Pack 1, 5-Part Blitz

The trombones most commonly seen in school settings are what kind of trombone?
tenor trombone
In what form of punishment was a citizen of ancient Athens expelled from the city for 10 years?
ostracism
Category

Set

A group of questions on a single announced subject. All open to both teams, 10 points each.

Example — Pack 1, Set of 4: Months

The ancient Roman month of Quintilis is now known by what name?
July
Which month is named for a two-faced Roman god?
January
Category — new for 2024-25

Mystery

A group of questions whose answers all fit an undisclosed theme. The subject is not announced — figuring it out is part of the fun. Open to both teams, 10 points each.

Example — Pack 3, 4-Part Mystery

What company's iconic product is a rectangular metal lighter with a hinged lid?
Zippo
Jewish communities in New York and Montreal developed different distinctive versions of what bread product?
bagels
What power forward joined Miami in 2023 after spending nine seasons with the Cleveland Cavaliers?
Kevin Love
During World War II, what model of fighter aircraft was heavily used by the Japanese Air Force?
Mitsubishi A6M Zero

Theme: every answer can mean zero / nothing (Zippo, bagel, "love" in tennis, Zero).

Category

Linked Set

Assorted-subject questions in which the answer to each question is somehow related to the next question. Open to both teams, 10 points each.

Note: in 2024-25 PS packs, Mystery and Linked Set are sometimes combined into a single category called "Mystery Link".

Category

Double Jump

Two thematically-linked questions: a first jump and a second jump.

  • The first jump is open to both teams.
  • If the first jump is answered correctly, the second jump goes only to the team that got it.
  • If no one gets the first jump, the second jump is still open to both teams.
  • Each jump is worth 10 points.

Example — Pack 1

1st jump. Glucose is composed of what three elements?
carbon AND oxygen AND hydrogen
2nd jump. A glucose molecule with what additional element in place of one of the hydroxyl groups is used as a common positron source for PET scans?
fluorine
Category

Streak

Name multiple items that satisfy some given criteria.

  • The first player to buzz gives answers one at a time until they make a mistake or pass ("done"/"pass").
  • Wrong or repeated answer → 0 points for that team. Otherwise, 5 points per correct answer.
  • The other team may then buzz and name any remaining answers under the same scoring.

Example — Pack 1

Name up to any five of the individuals voted by the public as the top 10 Greatest Canadians in a 2004 series produced by the CBC.
Tommy Douglas; Terry Fox; Pierre Trudeau; Frederick Banting; David Suzuki; Lester B. Pearson; Don Cherry; John A. Macdonald; Alexander Graham Bell; Wayne Gretzky
Category

Jailbreak

At least 12 assorted-subject questions, open to both teams. Each correct answer earns 10 points — but:

  • Once you answer one correctly, you can no longer buzz.
  • Once all four players on a team have each answered one, the team "breaks out" and everyone may buzz again.
  • No consultation at all during a Jailbreak — verbal or non-verbal. Signals or gestures to teammates before buzzing are treated as a wrong answer.
Category

Split

Two sets of four questions, each on its own announced subject. Active players pair up into two duos — one duo per subject.

  • Subjects are announced before assigning duos. No prior coordination on possible answers.
  • Only the assigned duo may buzz on their subject. Consultation must be out loud — no whispering.
  • 10 points per question.

Example — Pack 1, Splits: Pokémon & Antarctica

Pokémon. What Pokémon can never have more than one HP due to the effects of its ability Wonder Guard?
Shedinja
Antarctica. Air New Zealand Flight 901 crashed into what mountain, the highest active volcano in Antarctica?
Mount Erebus
Category

Jackpot

A single answer revealed across four parts of descending difficulty. Each team gets up to two buzzes total across all four parts.

Part 140 points
Part 230 points
Part 320 points
Part 410 points

Example — Pack 1

Part 1 (40). What was the first name of a goalie suspended from their national team for saying "You can't live by big names. You can't live in the past" after being benched for a 4-0 loss to Brazil?
Part 2 (30). This English word titles the Israeli national anthem, known in Hebrew as "Hatikvah".
Part 3 (20). In one poem, Emily Dickinson described this as "A thing with feathers".
Part 4 (10). According to Greek myth, this was the only thing that remained after Pandora's Box was opened.
hope

If you've played quizbowl…

three things to keep in mind.

1.

You must buzz to answer every question.

There are no "bonuses" you just speak the answer to.

  • Open questions: buzz before giving your answer.
  • Double Jumps: the second jump still requires a buzz, even when only your team can answer it.
  • If you start answering without buzzing, it counts as no answer.

2.

Confer freely — until someone buzzes.

Verbal or non-verbal consultation is allowed on every question.

  • Talk it out, point at notes, nod, whatever you like — until the buzz.
  • Once a player on your team buzzes, no more consultation — that player must give the answer.
  • Exception: Jailbreak forbids any consultation at all. Splits only allow consultation within your duo, out loud.

3.

Three seconds to buzz. One second to answer.

After the reader finishes the question:

  • 3 seconds to buzz (unless the question states otherwise).
  • After buzzing, 1 second to begin your answer.
  • If the first team buzzes incorrectly, the other team gets another 3 seconds.

We will be enforcing these times strictly.

Good luck.
