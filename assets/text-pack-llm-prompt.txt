============================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT
============================================================

The output is plain UTF-8 text. Each logical element goes on its own line.
Blank lines between categories are allowed (and encouraged for
readability). No leading whitespace on any line.

A. CATEGORY HEADERS
   - A category name appears on its own line, with no number prefix and
     no answer marker. Example:
       5-Part Blitz
   - Common category names you may see: "5-Part Blitz", "12-Part Blitz",
     "Double Jump", "Jackpot", "Streak", "Jailbreak", "Set of 3",
     "Set of 4", "Set of 5", "Splits: <Topic A> & <Topic B>". Use
     whatever the source provides; do not rename categories.
   - If the source has prose instructions for the category (e.g.,
     "Each answer is two phrases joined by a shared word."), put that
     prose on its own line(s) IMMEDIATELY AFTER the category header,
     BEFORE the first question.

B. QUESTIONS
   - Every question is one line starting with its number, a period, and
     a single space: `N. question text`
   - Questions are numbered 1..100 (or as numbered in the source).
   - The question text continues on the same line. Do NOT break a
     question across multiple lines.

C. ANSWERS
   - Each answer line starts with `A: ` (capital A, colon, single space):
       A: Shakespeare
   - One answer line immediately follows each question that has its own
     answer.

D. STREAKS (one prompt, many accepted answers)
   - One numbered question line, then one `A: ` line per accepted answer:
       Streak
       10. Name as many US state capitals as you can.
       A: Albany
       A: Boston
       A: Sacramento
   - The streak occupies the slots between its number and the next
     question's number (so Q10 with the next question being Q14 covers
     slots 10–13). Do not pad with extra numbered questions.

E. JACKPOTS (4 progressively-revealing clues, 1 shared answer)
   - Four consecutive numbered clue lines, then a single `A: ` line
     after the last clue:
       Jackpot
       14. Clue 1 text.
       15. Clue 2 text.
       16. Clue 3 text.
       17. Clue 4 text.
       A: shared answer

F. SPLITS (two sub-categories under one header)
   - The Splits header line names both sub-categories, joined by ` & `.
     Then each sub-category title appears on its own line, followed by
     its questions:
       Splits: Star Wars Lightsabers & Literature
       Star Wars Lightsabers
       26. ...
       A: ...
       27. ...
       A: ...
       Literature
       30. ...
       A: ...

G. STRUCTURAL TEXT TO DROP
   - Skip pack-level boilerplate that is not part of a question: page
     numbers, "PACK 1", "FIRST QUARTER", editor credits, copyright
     lines, scoring rubric headings, etc. These are NOT categories.
